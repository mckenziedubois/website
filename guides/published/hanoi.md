#### Hanoi Travel Guide

Hanoi, located in northern Vietnam, is an excellent place to begin your Vietnam adventure. Its winding, maze-like streets are filled with hidden food gems, shopping, and rich cultural history. It is the perfect place to get lost and very possibly have the best pho or bánh mì of your life.

#### Where to Stay in Hanoi

We stayed at the Amira Hotel Hanoi in the Old Quarter, and it was the perfect home base for exploring the city. We spent six nights there, five in a suite with a balcony and one in a more budget-friendly room after returning from our overnight cruise in Lan Ha Bay. The hotel offered an excellent buffet breakfast with both Western and traditional Vietnamese options, plenty of locally sourced fruit, and an omelet bar. There was also a rooftop bar that was ideal for grabbing a drink and enjoying views over the Old Quarter.

#### Historical and Cultural Highlights

- **Thang Long Water Puppet Theatre:** Buy tickets in advance if possible. We arrived early and were able to secure last-minute tickets, but it is very popular.
- **Vietnamese Women's Museum and Hoa Lo Prison**: Both were major highlights and conveniently located near each other.
- **Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum and Complex**: We did not go inside due to dress code restrictions, but the grounds are impressive.
- **Temple of Literature**: Beautiful and historic. It was especially fun to see students visiting on field trips who were eager to practice their English.
- **Tran Quoc Pagoda**: Located near the mausoleum, this lakeside pagoda is serene and well worth a visit.
- **Train Street**: This narrow alley alongside an active railway track. We waited a few hours to catch the train. Note that regulations have been changing, so do your research before visiting to confirm access and any rules.
- **Long Bien Bridge**: I would not recommend walking across it. While there is a sidewalk, it doesn't continue the full length of the bridge and is quite narrow. 

#### Overnight Trip to Lan Ha Bay

Northern Vietnam offers several multi-day excursions from Hanoi. We did not want to miss the limestone cliffs and dramatic landscapes east of the city, so we booked an overnight cruise to Lan Ha Bay with Doris Cruise. Lan Ha Bay sits adjacent to the more famous Ha Long Bay but is slightly less crowded for now. 

We were picked up in the Old Quarter and driven about three hours to the coast, where we boarded the ship. We booked a few weeks in advance, and the only available suite happened to be the honeymoon suite, so we were very well taken care of. The cruise included swimming, kayaking, squid fishing, and guided excursions to other parts of the national park. It was a highlight of our time in Vietnam.

#### Where to Eat in Hanoi

The short answer is everywhere. If you read my [Ho Chi Minh City guide](/guides/hcmcity), you know I believe the smaller the seat, the better the pho, and that definitely holds true in Hanoi. I had pho or bánh mì nearly every day, and I encourage you to do the same. Make sure to try Chin-Su chili sauce and properly douse your bánh mì. It is a game changer.

Hanoi is also an exceptional coffee city, with hundreds of cafés. It is especially known for egg coffee, so do not miss trying it at Cafe Giang or at any local café you come across. Our favorite coffee shop was THAIYEN CAFE Quán Thánh, which is included in my Google Maps guide and featured in my photo carousel. Their coconut coffee was outstanding. Unlike the slushier versions we encountered further south in places like Da Nang and Ho Chi Minh City, Thaiyen’s version was creamy, milky, and perfectly balanced. They also have ample seating in the corridor next to the café.

Plan to try and possibly become obsessed with all three iconic Vietnamese coffee styles: egg, coconut, and salt.

#### Planning Your Trip to Hanoi

Hanoi is a fantastic entry point into Vietnam, especially if you are traveling from Japan or Korea, as it is one of the closest Southeast Asian destinations. A couple of places we did not get to visit but would love to next time are Sa Pa, known for its rice terraces and mountainous scenery, and Ha Giang, famous for its multi-day motorbike loop through dramatic mountain landscapes. 

Hanoi sets the tone beautifully for a Vietnam itinerary. It is historic, energetic, and completely unforgettable.
